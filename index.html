How did Air Jordan start?

The history of Jordan shoes is intertwined with the legacy of Michael Jordan, one of the greatest basketball players of all time. It all began in 1984 when Nike signed a young rookie named Michael Jordan to a groundbreaking endorsement deal. This deal resulted in the creation of the Air Jordan line, a brand of basketball shoes and athletic apparel named after Jordan himself.


The first Air Jordan sneaker, the Air Jordan 1, was released in 1985. Designed by Peter Moore, it featured a distinctive high-top silhouette, Nike Air cushioning, and the iconic "Wings" logo. The shoe immediately gained attention for its bold design and performance on the court.


Beyond basketball, Air Jordans became a cultural phenomenon, influencing fashion and streetwear trends around the world. Collaborations with artists, designers, and celebrities further cemented the brand's status as a symbol of style and excellence.
